# Segments & Strategy Map — the spine + facets model, and finding gaps

> Part of the creative-strategy framework set. The compact index lives in the
> `exodus-strategist` skill; this file is the depth. Strategy is *what to make* — and
> **segments are the biggest unlock.** A new slice nobody targets wins even with imperfect
> copy, because the algorithm and the audience both reward a message they haven't seen.
> "Making is cheap now. Choosing what to make is the job."

This is the **Strategy** layer of the wheel (Strategy → Analysis → Ideation → Copy →
Editing → Creative → Iterations). Strategy governs everything downstream: it doesn't turn
with every cycle, it sets the map the rest of the work fills in.

---

## A segment is a slice; a persona is that slice as a person
Don't let "our 6 personas" cap you — there are thousands of slices. A brand profile's named
personas are a starting set, not the ceiling. The job is to keep finding slices nobody is
serving yet.

## Spine + facets (not an N-cube)
Model the market as an **outcome spine** with **facets hanging off it** — not a giant grid
of every-attribute-times-every-other (that explodes into noise).

- **Outcome is the spine** (the vertical axis) — the desire or pain. This is the chunkiest,
  primary cut. Go narrower underneath it (joint pain → knee, hip). Add as many outcomes as
  the market has.
- **Demographic crosses** (the horizontal axis) — gender, age, identity.
- **Qualifiers are filters** that thin a slice rather than define it:
  - **Mechanism** = *universal, mass-market beliefs ONLY* (cortisol, testosterone — what the
    whole market already believes causes the problem). This is **not** your product's own
    proprietary mechanism (that lives in the copy, as Curiosity — see `copy-blocks.md`).
  - **Condition** = a fact that limits fit (vegetarian, GLP-1 user, pregnant). *Stress-test
    it* — copy can fall inside a condition's bucket without the condition being the actual
    carve-out the ad is built on.
  - **Value** = a soft preference (organic-only, no-needle).

Clean rule for the blurry ones:
**Demographic** = what they ARE · **Identity** = who they SAY they are ·
**Condition** = a fact that limits fit · **Value** = what they PREFER.

A **persona** = an outcome × a specific set of facets, named.

## Two ways to add a new segment
1. **New outcome / pain point** → add a column (grow the spine).
2. **Go narrower** → add a condition or sub-slice inside one you already have.

## Awareness is the layer ABOVE — not a facet
Unaware · Problem-aware · Solution-aware · Product-aware · Most-aware. Collapse to working
buckets (Unaware/Problem · Solution/Product · Most-aware). The difference between awareness
levels is mostly the **hook and the entry point**; the underlying job is the same (install
mechanism → create desire → bridge to product). Keep awareness *out* of the segment cube or
you double-count the same slice five times. (Routing detail: `awareness-framework.md`.)

## Coverage & gaps — the Strategy Map
The map = all possible slices (the terrain, with no holes). It only becomes useful when you
overlay reality:

1. **Map the terrain** — outcomes (spine) × facets, with awareness as the layer above.
2. **Overlay your real ads** — classify each *whole* ad to the one persona it actually lands
   on (its net impression, not every attribute it touches). Shade what's covered.
3. **Empty cells = gaps.** That's the live output: a ranked list of slices you could own.

**Rank gaps with Chad logic** (judgment): gap size · urgency of the outcome (usually pain) ·
TAM · what competitors are doing / what's already working · zeitgeist. Then blend with
**taste** — the intuition beneath words, built by saturating in the actual accounts.
Judgment + taste, fed back every cycle, compound into **instinct** (the 1-of-1 read no bot
can hand you). That compounding is the human half of the dual flywheel.

## Finding new segments (process)
Pull real market language — customer reviews, ad comments, competitor swipes (`swipe` writes
fresh ads off a single competitor ad; the brand's competitor library feeds the rest) — read
it until you can feel the specific outcomes and desires underneath, then extract them into a
master list of candidate segments. New outcomes grow the spine; new conditions grow the
facets. The point isn't perfect taxonomy — it's surfacing the slice nobody's serving.

## Output of this layer
A **Strategy Map** (the living terrain + the ranked gaps) and, feeding into Analysis, a
sense of where to plant the flag this cycle. Hand the chosen gaps to Ideation (`idea`,
STORMING) and they become briefs.
