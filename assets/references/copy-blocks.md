# Copy Blocks — P3C2 (the building blocks of persuasion)

> Part of the creative-strategy framework set; compact index lives in `exodus-strategist`.
> The five Copy Blocks are the "lego blocks" of copy. Treat them as **thinking tools, not a
> procedure** — master them until they're automatic, then throw away the ladder. *Clarity is
> king: never sacrifice clarity for something cute.* You improve almost ANY copy two ways:
> **add a missing block** or **make an existing block stronger.** ~90% of all copy feedback
> reduces to one of those two moves — including the feedback you'll give a Genesis draft.

> **P3C2 = Pain · Promise · Proof · Constraints · Curiosity** (+ a bonus **Conditions** block).

**The metaphor:** the prospect is in *Pain Country* and wants the *Promised Land* across the
canyon. **Curiosity** is the helicopter that carries them across · **Proof** is the pilot
(they won't board if they don't trust it) · **Constraints** are the baggage they must drop ·
**Conditions** is the dynamite set to blow if they don't get in now.

**The Persuasion Equation:** `VALUE = PROMISE × (PROOF × CURIOSITY) / CONSTRAINTS × CONDITIONS`.
Use it as a scoring lens on any draft: which term is weak, which is missing, which is
carrying the piece.

---

## 1 · PAIN — what they want to get RID of
An urgent problem negatively affecting their life that they desperately want solved. Use
**problem tokens** — the symptoms as they're *lived* ("messy carpets," not "digestive
issues").

**The Pain Chain** — expressions sequenced to warm up and "turn the knife":
General → Specific → Specific → Specific → **Cinematic** (a concrete movie of the pain in
daily life) → **Emotional** (the inner feeling) → **Core Wound** (the deepest existential
fear). *"The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel understood."* — Hormozi

## 2 · PROMISE — what they want to GAIN
The reversal of the pain into the desired outcome / identity gain. **Promise Ladder:**
General → Specific ×3 → **Cinematic** → **Emotional** → **Core Transformation.** Two rules:
**(1) it's hard to overdo Promises** — most people underdo them; be generous, lather them on
(Promise raises the energy of the copy). **(2) Always anchor to the one Core Transformation**
they're really after. Live on the **edge of believability** — almost too good to be true,
but plausible enough they *want* to believe.

## 3 · PROOF — reason to believe
*"Proof is more important than promise."* — Bencivenga. It's about creating the **feeling**
of proof, not winning a legal argument.
- **Siamese Twin:** pair every Promise with Proof right after it.
- **Balance Scale:** *never make the claim bigger than the proof.* Big promise → big proof.
  (This is the same instinct as the "proof placement follows claim strength" quality bar:
  outlandish claims need proof immediately, in the hook itself.)
- **5 categories / 22 types:** **Psychological** (jargon, candid communication, explanatory
  reasoning, logic, guarantees) · **Experiential** (testable, demos, trials/samples,
  challenges, comparisons, before/after) · **Empirical** (studies, stats, infographics) ·
  **Credible** (certifications, track record, expert endorsements, media, awards) · **Social**
  (testimonials/UGC, case studies, engagement).
- **Rules of thumb:** match proof level to promise level · hard-to-fake = stronger ·
  **Experiential is the strongest** (people draw their own conclusion — show, don't tell) ·
  **Psychological is the most useful + underused** (high strength-to-effort, grounded in
  perception). And the standing rule: **verify every proof point a bot hands you** — Genesis
  fabricates studies and stats; fact-check before anything ships.

## 4 · CONSTRAINTS — what holds them back (dissolve these)
Objections/restrictions that inhibit the sale — conscious and subconscious. **The Big Three:
Money · Time · Effort** (minimize all of them).
- **The Resonance Hierarchy** — how constraints get built: **Experiences → Beliefs → Values
  → Identity** (formed bottom-up). But prospects **interact top-down**, and **Identity is the
  most crystallized** → if your message conflicts with their identity, you trigger an
  autopilot rejection. So **appease Identity, avoid conflicting with Values**, then rework
  the pyramid from the ground up. *Molding identities is literally how you "build buyers"* —
  people buy to reinforce who they are or to become who they want to be. Experiences/Beliefs
  are the malleable playground.
- **Dissolve via A.W.E.:** **Acknowledge** the belief and its validity ("I used to think
  that too, and it makes sense…") → **Wedge** a counterexample that creates a *both/and*
  reality (not either/or) → **Elaborate** the new option. Counterexample types: scientific
  evidence · news · history · anecdotes · analogies · paradoxical questions.

## 5 · CURIOSITY (Mechanism) — the bridge from Pain → Promise
The most important and most differentiated block — where you draw out what's unique. **"Curiosity
is a new, simple, unique, exciting way to go from Pain to Promise."** Lives in headlines,
hooks, fascinations, mechanisms, and names. (This is the home of UMP/UMS — the unique
mechanism of the *problem* and of the *solution*; the named reason this works when
everything else failed.) Make it strong on **two dimensions:**

- **INSIGHT** (the "aha"): **Simple · Easy · Explanatory · Novel.** *Simple* = few moving
  parts (rule of 1). *Easy* = feels easy to DO (the anti-constraint). *Explanatory* = a
  **bigger idea that eats the old one** (Einstein > Newton) — acknowledges the old idea's
  success, explains its failure, handles edge cases. *Novel* = new but not too far out — the
  **Epiphany Threshold** is ~level 6–8 of 10 (level 0 = everyone knows; level 10 = the
  bullshit-detector trips).
- **CHARACTERIZATION** (the name / how you frame it): **Evocative Naming** (reveal just
  enough — "Hidden Constipation," not "Gut Cleanse") · **Idea Caricatures** (follow
  associations/analogies to exaggerate the sexy/taboo/exciting aspects — the highest-leverage
  art) · **Intuition Pumps** (match the prospect's "body feel" — e.g. "Joint Drought" →
  "5-Second Joint Hydration Trick").
- **The Curiosity Quadrant** (where to pull the idea from): **External/Internal ×
  Problem/Solution × General/Specific** — External = what the market already believes,
  Internal = your new insight. Apply **rhetorical frames** to any belief — **Reversals · Gaps
  · Patterns · Mirroring · Simplifiers** — to spin hooks.

## Bonus · CONDITIONS — the offer terms
More a feature of the OFFER than the copy, but powerful when you have them: **Qualifications**
(call-out language) · **Conversion Triggers** (urgency/scarcity) · **Risk Reversal**
(guarantees, "you don't pay," money-back, trials) · **Value Adds** (bonuses/discounts) ·
**Terms & Structure** (payment plans, financing). Combine with Curiosity + Proof so the offer
doesn't sound like everyone else's.

---

## Making ANY block stronger — CRAVES
**Clear · Relevant · Accurate · Visual · Expressive · Specific.**
- **Accurate** is the big one — pull the market's *exact words* from reviews, comments,
  forums, calls. *"It's not how hard you hit; it's hitting the exact reflex point on the
  knee."*
- **Visual** — ~50% of the brain is visual; make Pain/Promise a movie they can watch.
- **Expressive** — use visceral, connotation-soaked language (power words) — *don't overdo
  it.*
- **Specific** — "$500–1,500/day" beats "profit"; "10 lbs of lean muscle" beats "build
  muscle."

## Copy Velocity — drench the copy, cut filler
`Copy Velocity = (# of blocks / # of words) × 100`. Higher = more concentrated. The best copy
DRENCHES itself in blocks and limits words not tied to one. (A metaphor, not an exact science
— clarity still wins.)

## How to apply
1. **Recognize** the blocks (and their absence) in any piece — including a Genesis draft.
2. **Add missing blocks** — e.g. a headline with only Pain+Promise gets stronger by adding
   Constraints ("…without cold outreach"), then Curiosity (a named method), then Credibility
   ("9-figure copywriter reveals…").
3. **Strengthen existing blocks** with CRAVES.

This is the vocabulary for *steering* a writing pass: instead of "make it better," name the
block. "Add Proof right after that Promise (Siamese Twin)." "The Curiosity is weak — give the
mechanism an evocative name." That's how you turn a bot draft into a finished ad.
