# Awareness Framework — The 5 Buckets

This is the awareness-level framework for the Genesis writing process. Every brief gets classified into one of these five levels. The classification sets the starting place of the ad and which primer Genesis uses.

**The Critical Rule:** The job is ALWAYS the same — install the mechanism → create desire → bridge to product. The ONLY difference between awareness levels is the starting place. Don't treat them as different jobs. Same job, different entry point.

> **Exodus note on primers.** In this workspace, primers are **foundation fields**, not files. The cold primer is `primerUnawareProblemAware` (unaware + problem-aware winning ads); the warm primer is `primerSolutionProductAware` (solution + product-aware winning ads). Set them with the `exodus-foundation` skill. `--awareness <level>` on a Genesis run selects which primer the bots receive.

---

## Bucket 1: UNAWARE — Surface What's Hidden

### Core Jobs:
- Bring "symptom" to surface
- Bring desire for a new opportunity to surface
- Create dissatisfaction/instability with the status quo

### What This Means:
The reader doesn't know they have a problem, or doesn't know a solution category exists. You start with raw curiosity, symptom, or problem.

### Towards (Opportunity):
Surface an opportunity they don't know exists. The ad reveals something new — a possibility, a hidden advantage, a discovery — that creates desire from scratch.

### Away (Problem):
Surface a threat they don't know exists. The ad reveals a hidden danger, a silent symptom, an invisible risk — creating urgency from awareness itself.

### Example Hooks:
- "How are ancient teeth — buried for thousands of years — still perfectly preserved… while ours rot even though we brush twice a day?" (paradox → hidden mechanism)
- "Watch what nuts do to your body. Cashews aren't actually nuts — they're part of the poison ivy family." (pure education frame)

### Primer: `primerUnawareProblemAware` (cold)

---

## Bucket 2: PROBLEM AWARE — Align With the Problem Model (UMP)

### Core Jobs:
- Move them from SYMPTOM to your ROOT CAUSE (it's not X, it's Y)
- Install your Unique Mechanism of the Problem (UMP)
- Undo/accommodate false beliefs about other "models"

### What This Means:
The reader knows they have a problem but doesn't know what's really causing it. You start with their known problem and reframe the cause.

### Towards (Root Factor of Success):
Show them the root factor behind the results they want — the thing they've been missing.

### Away (Root Cause):
Reframe from the surface symptom to the real root cause — the mechanism they didn't know about.

### Example Hooks:
- "This will make you stop snoring instantly. If you don't fix snoring, you'll have the same fate as Margaret Thatcher." (reframes "annoying noise" → "your brain is suffocating 80 times a night")
- "Paying your bills on time raises your credit score, right. Wrong." (installs a new belief about the real cause)

### Primer: `primerUnawareProblemAware` (cold)

---

## Bucket 3: SOLUTION AWARE — Align With the Solution Model (UMS)

### Core Jobs:
- Get them to accept your SPECIFIC APPROACH as right
- Install your Unique Mechanism of the Solution (UMS)
- Prove differentiation and superiority

### What This Means:
The reader knows solutions exist. They're comparing options. You start with the solution category and prove yours is best.

### Towards (Your Path to the Opportunity):
Position your solution as the clearest, fastest, most reliable route to the outcome they want.

### Away (Your Fix for Their Problem):
Systematically dismantle alternatives and position yours as the only one addressing root cause.

### Example Hooks:
- "This is the simplest way to silence snoring at home. I tried these popular anti-snoring solutions so you don't have to." (dismantles each alternative, then positions the product)
- "Just 12 minutes a day with this NASA-inspired device gave me my hairline back." (differentiates through mechanism + authority)

### Primer: `primerSolutionProductAware` (warm)

---

## Bucket 4: PRODUCT AWARE — Win the Comparison

### Core Jobs:
- Make yours the OBVIOUS CHOICE
- Create a ton of vendor trust
- Prove differentiation and superiority (own the category)

### What This Means:
The reader knows your product exists. They're deciding between you and alternatives.

### Towards (Best Path to Success):
Focus on what makes your product uniquely effective — features, results, social proof.

### Away (Best Fix Available):
Compare directly against named alternatives and win on every dimension that matters.

### Primer: `primerSolutionProductAware` (warm)

---

## Bucket 5: MOST AWARE — Remove Friction

### Core Jobs:
- Give them a reason to ACT NOW
- Create urgency, scarcity
- Remove all friction

### Towards (Opportunity-Based Urgency):
Limited-time bonus, exclusive access, the cost of waiting in terms of missed benefits.

### Away (Problem-Based Urgency):
Every day without the product, the problem compounds. The cost of inaction is specific and measurable.

### Primer: none needed (Most Aware ads are direct offers)

---

## Towards vs Away Quick Reference

| Bucket | Towards | Away |
|--------|---------|------|
| Unaware | Surface an opportunity they don't know exists | Surface a threat they don't know exists |
| Problem Aware | Explain what creates success | Explain what causes their problem |
| Solution Aware | Show your path to the opportunity | Show your fix for their problem |
| Product Aware | Prove you're the best path to success | Prove you're the best fix available |
| Most Aware | Create opportunity-based urgency | Create problem-based urgency |

**Rules:** Choose ONE primer per run. Never mix awareness buckets in a single run. The job is always install mechanism → create desire → bridge to product; only the starting place changes.

## The 7 Critical Beliefs (Primary Targets)

Every sale requires belief transformation. The most powerful beliefs to target:

1. **UNIQUE MECHANISM** — OLD: "My problem is caused by [surface symptom]" → NEW: "It's caused by [specific mechanism] that only this addresses"
2. **FAILED ALTERNATIVES** — OLD: "I've tried everything" → NEW: "What I tried was doomed because it didn't address [mechanism]"
3. **ESCALATING COST** — OLD: "This is manageable" → NEW: "This is actively getting worse and costing me daily"
4. **CONSTRAINT REMOVAL** — OLD: "I don't have time/money/discipline" → NEW: "I already have everything needed"
5. **PERSONAL AUTHORITY** — OLD: "Another guru selling snake oil" → NEW: "This person discovered it through struggle"
6. **RISK REVERSAL** — OLD: "I'll waste my money" → NEW: "This works or costs nothing"
7. **NOW-OR-NEVER** — OLD: "Maybe later" → NEW: "This opportunity won't remain available"
